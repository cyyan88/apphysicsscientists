Historical Scientists

Austria

Schrödinger’s Wave Equation, which gives the position of a particle (electron) at a certain position, was his most important contribution. This is important because the equation was the first to effectively describe the behavior of an electron and can be applied to both physics and chemistry, such as in creating new chemicals and medicines.

Schrödinger started seriously researching in 1920, which was post World War I, after serving as an officer. He also researched through WWII, where he escaped to Italy before returning to Italy later in his life.

New Zealand and England

Rutherford’s most important contribution was the discovery of radioactivity and alpha and beta radiation. This is important because radiation is used today in medicine, and knowledge that radiation exists highlights the danger of nuclear disasters and certain chemicals.

Rutherford’s research was done during the Industrial Revolution and World War I. He also worked with scientists such as Thomson and Bohr during this time period.
